Research

My mathematical interests sit at the intersection of analytic number theory, formal verification, and the emerging field of AI for mathematics.

Analytic Number Theory

Sums of two squares, gaps between arithmetic sequences, L-functions and zeta functions. Summer research at Oxford (2021, 2022) supervised by Dr Thomas Bloom (Royal Society Research Fellow) and Dr Tiago Jardim da Fonseca. Graduate coursework at Bonn includes the Smooth Circle Method (Heath-Brown).

Formal Verification & AI for Mathematics

Formalisation of graduate-level mathematics in Lean 4. Currently studying under Floris van Doorn (lead Mathlib contributor) at Bonn. Building BMUCO's Lean formalisation pipeline — DRP + workshops producing human-verified, graduate-level formal proof datasets. Scientific advisor: Prof. Yang-Hui He (Oxford / LIMS).

Quantitative Methods

Probability, measure theory, and decision-making under uncertainty. Selected for Jane Street's Quantitative Trading Camp (Spring 2024, London) and invited to their internship process. Interested in the formal mathematical foundations of quantitative finance.

Building the ecosystem for the future of sciences

Since 2019, I've co-built BMUCO alongside like-minded university students into a global science organisation. We run research mentorships, winter schools, and talks — and have built partnerships with leading institutions across Europe.

  • Co-hosted Abel Symposium 2025 with Norwegian Academy of Science and Letters and Hausdorff Center for Mathematics
  • Public interviews with Fields Medalists and Nobel Laureates
  • Research programmes reaching 20,000+ students globally
  • DAAD STIBET Scholar — competitive merit scholarship, University of Bonn
  • Full funding: AI×Mathematics Conference (ICMS Edinburgh, 2026)
  • Full funding: Isaac Newton Institute conference

Current focus: Lean Formalisation Initiative

BMUCO is building the first structured student pipeline for expert-verified, graduate-level formal mathematics datasets in Lean 4 — feeding the next generation of AI reasoning models. We run a Directed Reading Programme (DRP) followed by intensive workshops at Bonn, producing human-verified proof corpora and an open-source graduate autoformaliser (Ramanujan).
